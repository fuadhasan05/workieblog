| Time | Block Type | Activities |
|------|-----------|------------|
| 8:00-9:00 | üåÖ Morning Startup | Email, planning, light tasks |
| 9:00-11:30 | üß† Deep Work | Most challenging/important work |
| 11:30-12:00 | üìû Communication | Calls, messages, quick meetings |
| 12:00-1:00 | üçΩÔ∏è Lunch Break | Step away from your desk! |
| 1:00-3:00 | ü§ù Collaboration | Meetings, team work |
| 3:00-4:30 | üîß Execution | Tasks, follow-ups |
| 4:30-5:00 | üìã Wrap-up | Tomorrow's prep, final emails |

### The Pomodoro Technique
1. Choose a task
2. Set timer for 25 minutes
3. Work with full focus
4. Take 5-minute break
5. After 4 pomodoros, take 15-30 minute break

### Eat the Frog
Do your most dreaded or important task first thing in the morning when willpower is highest.

## üéØ Daily Routines

### Morning Routine (Before Work)
- [ ] Wake at consistent time
- [ ] Exercise or stretch (even 10 minutes helps)
- [ ] Shower and get dressed (no pajamas!)
- [ ] Eat a proper breakfast
- [ ] Review your day's priorities

### Start-of-Work Ritual
1. Make your morning beverage ‚òï
2. Review calendar and to-do list
3. Set your 3 MIT (Most Important Tasks)
4. Close unnecessary tabs and apps
5. Put phone on Do Not Disturb
6. Start your first focus block

### End-of-Work Ritual
1. Review what you accomplished
2. Note any carryover tasks
3. Set top 3 priorities for tomorrow
4. Send any final messages
5. Close work applications
6. Physical "shutdown" - leave your workspace

## üí¨ Communication Best Practices

### Async vs. Sync Communication
| Async (Preferred) | Sync (When Needed) |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Slack/Teams messages | Video calls |
| Email | Phone calls |
| Recorded videos (Loom) | Live collaboration |
| Shared documents | Urgent issues |

### Writing Effective Messages
```
üìå Subject: [Clear topic] - [Action needed/FYI]

Context: Brief background (1-2 sentences)

Request/Update: What you need or are sharing

Details: Supporting information (bullet points)

Next Steps: Clear action items with owners and deadlines

Timeline: When you need response/action
```

### Video Call Etiquette
- [ ] Test audio/video before important calls
- [ ] Mute when not speaking
- [ ] Look at camera (not screen) when talking
- [ ] Use virtual background if workspace is messy
- [ ] Minimize notifications during calls
- [ ] Be present - no multitasking!

## üßò Maintaining Work-Life Balance

### Setting Boundaries
1. **Physical boundaries:** Dedicated workspace you can leave
2. **Time boundaries:** Set and communicate work hours
3. **Digital boundaries:** Separate devices or profiles for work
4. **Social boundaries:** Communicate availability to household

### Avoiding Burnout
- Take all your breaks (including lunch!)
- Use your PTO - remote work burnout is real
- Don't check work messages outside hours
- Build in buffer time between meetings
- Say no to unnecessary meetings

### Movement Throughout the Day
- Stand up every hour
- Walking meetings when possible
- Stretch between tasks
- Exercise during lunch break
- Consider a standing desk

## üìä Measuring Your Productivity

### Daily Check-In Questions

- Did I maintain my boundaries?

- Deep work hours per day
- Meeting hours per week
- Response time to important messages

## üì± Digital Wellness

### Reducing Screen Fatigue
- Follow 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, look 20 ft away for 20 sec)
- Use blue light filtering
- Adjust monitor brightness to match surroundings
- Take "camera off" calls when possible
- Schedule screen-free breaks

### Notification Management
- Turn off non-essential notifications
- Set "Focus" modes on devices
- Check email at set times, not constantly
- Use "Do Not Disturb" during deep work
