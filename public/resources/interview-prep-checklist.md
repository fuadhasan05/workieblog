# Ultimate Interview Preparation Checklist

## 1 Week Before

### Company Research
- [ ] Study the company's website, mission, and values
- [ ] Read recent news articles about the company
- [ ] Review their social media presence
- [ ] Understand their products/services
- [ ] Research their competitors
- [ ] Look up the interviewer(s) on LinkedIn

### Role Preparation
- [ ] Re-read the job description thoroughly
- [ ] Identify key skills and requirements
- [ ] Prepare examples that demonstrate each requirement
- [ ] Research typical salary range for the role

### Logistics
- [ ] Confirm interview date, time, and location
- [ ] Plan your route (add 30 min buffer)
- [ ] Prepare outfit and ensure it's clean/pressed
- [ ] Test video/audio equipment if virtual interview

---

## 3 Days Before

### Practice Sessions
- [ ] Practice answers to common questions (see below)
- [ ] Prepare 5-7 STAR method stories
- [ ] Record yourself and review
- [ ] Do a mock interview with a friend

### Materials
- [ ] Print 5 copies of your resume
- [ ] Prepare a list of references
- [ ] Gather work samples/portfolio if applicable
- [ ] Prepare questions to ask the interviewer

---

## Day Before

- [ ] Get a good night's sleep (7-8 hours)
- [ ] Lay out your outfit
- [ ] Pack your bag with all materials
- [ ] Review your notes one final time
- [ ] Set multiple alarms

---

## Day Of

### Morning
- [ ] Eat a healthy breakfast
- [ ] Review key points (don't over-study)
- [ ] Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- [ ] Use the restroom before
- [ ] Silence your phone

### Mindset
- [ ] Take deep breaths
- [ ] Remember: they want you to succeed
- [ ] You're interviewing them too
- [ ] Smile and make eye contact

---

## Common Interview Questions to Prepare

### About You
1. Tell me about yourself
2. Why are you interested in this role?
3. Why are you leaving your current job?
4. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
5. What are your greatest strengths/weaknesses?

### Behavioral (STAR Method)
1. Tell me about a time you faced a challenge
2. Describe a situation where you showed leadership
3. Give an example of a goal you achieved
4. Tell me about a time you failed
5. Describe a conflict with a coworker and how you resolved it

### Role-Specific
1. What relevant experience do you have?
2. How would you approach [specific scenario]?
3. What would your first 90 days look like?

---

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer

1. What does success look like in this role?
2. What are the biggest challenges facing the team?
3. How would you describe the team culture?
4. What opportunities are there for growth?
5. What's the typical career path for someone in this role?
6. What do you enjoy most about working here?
7. What are the next steps in the interview process?

---

## STAR Method Framework

**S** - Situation: Set the scene
**T** - Task: Describe your responsibility
**A** - Action: Explain what you did
**R** - Result: Share the outcome (quantify if possible)

### Example:
"In my previous role (S), I was tasked with improving customer satisfaction scores that had dropped 15% (T). I implemented a new feedback system and trained the team on response protocols (A). Within 3 months, satisfaction scores improved by 25% (R)."

---

## Post-Interview

- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Connect with interviewers on LinkedIn
- [ ] Note what went well and areas to improve
- [ ] Follow up if you haven't heard back in stated timeframe
